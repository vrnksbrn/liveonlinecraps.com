A Deep Dive into the Game: Rules, Bets, and Strategy

Craps can appear daunting due to the complex-looking table, but the core game is built on a simple premise. The digital interface of a live casino craps online game helps enormously by highlighting valid bets and guiding you through the game’s phases.

The Foundation: The Come-Out Roll and Establishing the Point

Every round of craps begins with a Come-Out Roll. This is the first roll of the dice in a new round.

  • If the result is a 7 or 11 (a “Natural”), the most common bet, the Pass Line, wins instantly.

  • If the result is a 2, 3, or 12 (“Craps”), the Pass Line bet loses.

  • If the result is a 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, or 10, that number becomes the “Point.” The dealer places a puck marked “ON” on that number.

Once the Point is established, the game’s objective shifts. The goal is now to roll that Point number again before a 7 is rolled. If the Point hits, Pass Line bets win. If a 7 (the “Seven-Out”) appears first, Pass Line bets lose, and the round ends.

The Core Wagers: Where Every Beginner Should Start

For your first session at a casino craps live dealer online table, focus on these fundamental bets.

  • Pass Line Bet: You’re betting with the shooter. You win on a 7 or 11 on the come-out, and you win if the Point is rolled before a 7. This is the most popular bet and has a low house edge of 1.41%.

  • Don’t Pass Bet: You’re betting against the shooter. You win on a 2 or 3 on the come-out (12 is a push) and win if a 7 is rolled before the Point. This has a slightly better house edge of 1.36%.

  • The Odds Bet: This is the single most important bet in craps. After a Point is established, you can place an additional bet behind your Pass Line bet. This bet has ZERO house edge. It pays true odds, meaning the casino has no advantage. Always take the maximum odds allowed; it is the smartest move you can make.

Expanding Your Play: Intermediate Bets

Once you’re comfortable, you can explore other wagers to make the game more dynamic.

  • Come Bets: A Come bet works like a new Pass Line bet made after the main Point is on. The next roll acts as the come-out for your bet.

  • Place Bets: A direct bet that a specific number (e.g., 6 or 8) will be rolled before a 7. These are great for having action on multiple numbers.
