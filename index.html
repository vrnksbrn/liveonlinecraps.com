The Definitive Guide: How to Play Craps Live Online

Craps can seem intimidating at first glance due to the complex-looking table layout and the flurry of different bets. However, the game’s core mechanics are surprisingly simple. When you play live dealer craps online, the interface helps immensely, highlighting valid bets and guiding you through the process. Let’s break it down.

The Game Flow: The Come-Out Roll and The Point

Every round of craps begins with a “Come-Out Roll.” This is the first roll of the dice by the “Shooter” (in a land-based casino, players take turns being the shooter; online, the dealer is always the shooter).

  • Before the Come-Out Roll: Players place their bets. The most fundamental bet is the Pass Line bet.

  • The Roll: The dealer throws the dice.

    • If the result is a 7 or 11 (a “Natural”), all Pass Line bets instantly win. The round is over, and a new Come-Out Roll begins.

    • If the result is a 2, 3, or 12 (“Craps”), all Pass Line bets instantly lose. The round is over, and a new Come-Out Roll begins.

    • If the result is a 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, or 10, that number becomes the “Point.” The dealer will place a puck (usually marked “ON”) on that number on the table. The game now enters its second phase.

  • After the Point is Established: The goal for Pass Line bettors is now for the dealer to roll the Point number again before rolling a 7.

    • If the Point number is rolled, Pass Line bets win. The round ends.

    • If a 7 is rolled (a “Seven-Out”), Pass Line bets lose. The round ends.

The Core Bets: Pass Line and Don’t Pass Line

These are the two foundational bets in craps and the best place for any beginner to start. They offer some of the lowest house edges in the entire casino.

  • Pass Line Bet: You are betting with the shooter. You win on a 7 or 11 on the Come-Out roll and lose on a 2, 3, or 12. If a Point is established, you win if the Point is rolled again before a 7. The house edge is a very low 1.41%.

  • Don’t Pass Line Bet: You are betting against the shooter. This is essentially the opposite of the Pass Line. You win if the Come-Out roll is a 2 or 3. A 12 is a “push” (your bet is returned). You lose if the Come-Out roll is a 7 or 11. If a Point is established, you win if a 7 is rolled before the Point. The house edge is slightly lower at 1.36%.

Expanding Your Horizons: Come and Don’t Come Bets

Once you’re comfortable with the Pass Line, you can explore Come and Don’t Come bets. These work almost identically to Pass Line and Don’t Pass bets, but you can place them after a Point has already been established.

  • Come Bet: When you place a Come bet, the very next roll of the dice acts as your personal Come-Out roll. If it’s a 7 or 11, you win immediately. If it’s a 2, 3, or 12, you lose. If it’s any other number (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10), that becomes your personal “Come Point,” and your bet moves to that number. You then win if your Come Point is rolled before a 7.

  • Don’t Come Bet: This is the opposite. After placing the bet, you win on a 2 or 3, push on a 12, and lose on a 7 or 11. If a number becomes your Don’t Come Point, you win if a 7 is rolled before that number.

These bets allow you to have multiple numbers working for you at once, making the game more dynamic.
